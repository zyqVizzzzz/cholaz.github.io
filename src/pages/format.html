From King to Exile to King Again
In a series of events that have sent shockwaves through the tech world, Sam Altman, the founder and CEO of OpenAI, was recently fired from the company, only to make a remarkable comeback. This report delves into the rollercoaster of events that unfolded at OpenAI, highlighting the complexities and internal dynamics of one of the most influential companies in the AI sector.

  • November 17th - Friday

    The Firing of Sam Altman

    On Friday, November 17th, at 12:19 PM, the OpenAI board delivered a shocking message to Sam Altman in the absence of Greg Brockman, the president of OpenAI, stating, "You're fired." The meeting abruptly ended.

    Minutes later, Greg Brockman received a Google Meet video call request from Ilya Sutskever. At 12:23 PM, a conference call began, announcing Brockman's removal from the board but his retention as president. The swift and precise execution of these actions indicated a premeditated move with no hesitation or protracted discussions.

    Rumors circulated that Sutskever, a co-founder and Chief Scientist at OpenAI, was the mastermind behind a coup, secretly aligning with three independent directors to decisively vote 4:2 in favor of ousting Altman. OpenAI's official website promptly announced changes in leadership, with a cautious and vague statement. The statement suggested that Altman's communication lacked transparency, hindering the board's ability to fulfill its duties and eroding confidence in his leadership.

    Simultaneously, OpenAI informed its major investor, Microsoft, of the leadership changes. Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella and other executives learned of this news just 5 to 10 minutes before the announcement, leaving them surprised.

  • November 17th - Friday

    Inside Story and Big Twist

    On Friday, November 17th, at 2 PM, OpenAI held an all-hands meeting to reassure employees and announced that CTO Murati would serve as interim CEO. However, Altman's departure was not without resistance. OpenAI's shareholders, including Airbnb CEO Brian Chesky and SV Angel founder Ron Conway, publicly voiced their support for Altman and Brockman.

    On Sunday, November 19th, in the morning, Altman and Brockman negotiated at OpenAI's San Francisco headquarters. The outcome of the negotiations was the appointment of Emmett Shear as CEO, not Altman. In the evening, OpenAI employees penned a strongly-worded open letter, threatening to resign and follow Altman and Brockman to Microsoft unless the board resigned and reinstated them.

  • April 2011 - heute

    Sam Altman Joins Microsoft

    On Monday, November 20th, in the afternoon, Satya Nadella explicitly expressed his welcome to Altman, Brockman, and other OpenAI employees on a media program, and announced that Microsoft had prepared laptops and GPU clusters at their LinkedIn office in San Francisco. However, due to ongoing negotiations between the board and OpenAI leadership, there were still no OpenAI employees at the office. It was understood that Microsoft's preference was for Altman to return as CEO to OpenAI.

    At midnight, Nadella officially tweeted the announcement of Altman joining Microsoft

  • April 2011 - heute

    Employees Force the Board of Directors

    Around 2 AM on November 21st, OpenAI's top management extended an olive branch to Altman, stating, "OpenAI Is Nothing Without Its People" Altman retweeted some of these messages, expressing support with heart emojis. The employees' emotions reached a new peak, and the most astonishing twist was that Ilya Sutskever, the main planner of the coup, reversed his stance. At 5 AM, Sutskever posted on X expressing regret for Altman's dismissal. Altman retweeted this post and added three heart emojis.

    Even at this point, interim CEO Shear threatened that if the board couldn't clearly communicate the reasons for Altman's dismissal, he would no longer serve as CEO.

  • April 2011 - heute

    Sam Altman's Return and Board Shakeup

    "We have reached a preliminary agreement for Altman to return as the CEO of OpenAI." As a prerequisite for Altman's return, the board underwent a major reshuffle.

    New board members included Bret Taylor, former Salesforce Co-CEO and current Facebook CTO responsible for long-term technical development and overseeing the development of News Feed, Search, and the Facebook platform. Larry Summers, former U.S. Treasury Secretary, also joined the board.
